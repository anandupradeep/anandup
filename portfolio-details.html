Enhancing Image Retrieval: CNN and Feature Fusion

Crime scene investigation (CSI) image retrieval plays a crucial role in identifying and analyzing evidence pivotal for solving various crimes. While Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) have shown remarkable efficacy in large-scale image database retrieval, their training on limited CSI image datasets poses challenges of overfitting. To address this, this paper proposes a novel approach that leverages transfer learning to cascade two CNN models and integrates their features with low-level image features for more comprehensive CSI image representation.

Initially, two pre-trained CNN models undergo fine-tuning using the target CSI image set. From these models, CNN features are extracted from the fully connected layers and concatenated to form high-level features for each image. Subsequently, these concatenated CNN features are fused with the low-level image features of the target dataset. The resulting fused image features are then utilized for image retrieval tasks.

Experimental evaluations conducted on a CSI image database demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm, particularly in scenarios with limited training data. Furthermore, experiments conducted on the GHIM-10K database validate the generalizability of the proposed approach, showcasing its robustness across diverse datasets. This research contributes to advancing CSI image retrieval methodologies, offering a promising solution for enhancing the accuracy and reliability of crime scene analysis and investigation.
